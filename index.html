The solar system has eight planets there are :

#1

Mercury

Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun, has extreme temperatures, lacks a significant atmosphere, and is small with a heavily cratered surface. It has a swift orbit, completing one revolution in 88 days.

#2

Venus

Venus, Earth's neighbor, has a thick, toxic atmosphere of carbon dioxide and sulfuric acid clouds. It's the hottest planet due to a runaway greenhouse effect and has a retrograde rotation.

#3

Earth

Earth, the third planet from the Sun, supports life with its diverse ecosystems and liquid water. It has a breathable atmosphere, moderate temperatures, and a unique combination of geology and biology.

#4

Mars

Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun, has a thin atmosphere, icy poles, and the largest volcano, Olympus Mons. Its reddish appearance comes from iron oxide, and it hosts potential for past life.

#5

Jupiter

Jupiter, a gas giant, is known for its Great Red Spot, numerous moons, and faint rings. Its immense gravity and powerful storms make it a fascinating subject of study.

#6

Saturn

Saturn, the ringed giant, captivates with its stunning rings and numerous moons. Its atmosphere, composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, harbors powerful storms and intriguing atmospheric phenomena.

#7

Uranus

Uranus, an ice giant, is unique with its tilted axis, causing extreme seasons. Its blue-green color comes from methane in the atmosphere, and it has faint rings and numerous moons.

#8

Neptune

Neptune, the distant blue giant, is known for its intense storms and strong winds. Its deep blue color is due to methane, and it features faint rings and 14 known moons.
